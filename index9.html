Lachish River in Ashdod

The Lachish River in the city of Ashdod separates the port of Ashdod from the northern part of the city. Along the south bank of the creek there is a very accessible hiking trail, which is part of Lachish Park. There is an asphalt path in the stream, next to it a white stone, grass and vegetation. Along the path are benches for the benefit of the disabled. The view is amazing and the atmosphere is calm and addictive. Bird lovers are guaranteed a special treat. The path reaches the estuary to the sea and from there continues to the sea promenade in Ashdod, the landscape changes of course, but the accessibility continues to it. You can park in the Mei Ami beach parking lot, or on the ORT road in front of the ORT Ashdod marine school.

A little about Lachish Park- The eastern part of the park remains in its original condition, the paths are unpaved and instead of trees and natural vegetation streams are abundant. The western part of the park - includes manicured lawns, seating areas, a small zoo with several animal corners, a petting zoo, and decorated sculptures and benches. A birding center has opened there, and with the birds living in the park are the Siskin, the lizard, the white-breasted kingfisher, the common falcon and the dwarf kingfisher. In addition, swamps, rabbits and other animals live in the park.

The beaches of the city of Eilat and other attractions

The shores of Eilat - Two of the four beaches declared in the city of Eilat are accessible (Rimonim Beach and Seagulls Beach) - with disabled parking, access roads, sheds and the possibility of entering the water with a floating chair. The rescuers received guidance and instruction to assist those who wished to do so. Details about the accessibility arrangements at the beaches can be found on the Eilat Municipality's website.

More attractions- Accessible restaurants - All restaurants in the city of Eilat are currently undergoing a process of accessibility checks and adjustments to the place. Among the restaurants now accessible: Aroma, BARBIQUA, Pago Pago, 9 Beach, Fish Market, Tomato Pizza, Broad Heart and more. The Underwater Observatory - an accessible attraction, the first in Eilat and a leader in the country. Along with the physical accessibility of the place, the training team also knows how to mark in sign language with deaf people who speak sign language.

Sea Bell - Accessible Cruise Ship - The cruise ship managed by Captain Shimshon Moshiach, the only one in the country that has the option to board the wheelchair, the ability to move on board, accessible toilets and a ladder that allows you to reach in a respectable and pleasant wheelchair to the two levels, and most amazing - There is a special leaflet, which allows people with disabilities to be lowered into the water for water activities.

Accessible taxis - Fattal Taxi Company is the first in Eilat to bring a taxi that allows travel with a wheelchair. The fare is at the standard taxi fare. Taxis comply with the new accessibility regulations. All taxi drivers in the city were instructed that it is their duty to transport passengers with wheelchairs (with the wheelchair in the trunk) and people with guide dogs. Cliffs Springs Nature Reserve

Einot Tzukim is the lowest nature reserve in the world. The central part is a public-absorbing reserve and covers an area of ​​about 500 dunams. In this part there are paddling and swimming pools full of natural spring water. In this part of the reserve all visitors are invited to relax, swim and walk independently and there is also the archeological site from the Second Temple period.

accessibility: There are three disabled parking spaces near the archeological site and 2 disabled parking spaces near the pools. Asphalt roads straight and wide from the parking lots to the archeological site and to the pools. The archeological site has large and clear explanatory signs and an accessible information store near the entrance gate. There are shade sheds and picnic tables accessible to wheelchair visitors and disabled toilets with standard hand grips and wheelchair space next to the toilet. Two pools on site - "Reed pool" - a paddling pool. "Tamar Pool" - a swimming pool supervised by a lifeguard. People with reduced mobility have a water drop-down leaflet. The pool is not active every day and it is advisable to be updated on the days and hours of operation of the pool by phone 02-9942355.

Where the place is located: North of the Dead Sea 3 km south of Kibbutz Kalia on Road 90. The cost of entering the site: adult 27 NIS, child 14 NIS, 50% discount for senior citizens.
