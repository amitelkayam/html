The Forest of Angels Shachariya

In the charming forest near Kiryat Gat there are game statues in the shape of an ant and grasshopper lizard that have been specially adapted by the sculptor Ruslan Sergeev for people with motor disabilities and visual impairments. The accessible car park is located next to an inactive observation tower and has picnic and water tables. Apart from the trails in the forest there are many other trails for a pleasant hike including bike paths and also a beautiful winter blossom of cyclamen. How to get there? The entrance to the forest is on the side of Route 35, between the 24-23 km mark.

Warrior Park in Carob Forest

The park was established for IDF disabled people so that it is accessible and suitable for hiking and walking. In the forest there are many carob trees and many other types of trees that provide shade. The park, located on about 300 dunams, includes many facilities, large tables, accessible walking paths between cisterns, accessible bicycle paths and fitness facilities. How to get there? From the Gedera-Kiryat Malachi road (Route 40), turn east at the Ram (Masmiya) junction and take Route 3 for about 700 meters. Turn right on road 383 towards Kfar Menachem and about two kilometers after the entrance to the kibbutz (between signs 7-8 km) turn south to Haruvit Forest.

Masada National Park

Above the Dead Sea, on the summit of an isolated cliff, lies Masada Fortress. The fortress became a symbol of Jewish determination in the face of foreign Roman rule. Masada Fortress was built by King Herod in 37 to 31 BC in case his Jewish subjects revolted. In 66 AD, with the outbreak of the Jewish revolt against Rome, a group of zealots led by Elazar ben Yair conquered the fortress. The group carried out raids and harassment of communities in the area. When the Romans entered Masada, they discovered that 1000 of his defenders had chosen to set the fort on fire and commit suicide. The suicide story of the defenders of Masada has become a myth, although there are still many controversial details in the story.

National Park Tour: The tour of Masada begins at the modern visitor center established east of the fortress, where you can watch a film describing the history of Masada, be impressed by a model of the fortress and see archeological exhibits discovered at the site. From here you can continue to the cable car that will lead us to the heights of the fortress. The route: Inside the fort you can make a circular route along its impressive walls, and visit the magnificent palaces that Herod built, the food warehouses, the cisterns and the bathhouses.
