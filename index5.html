Accessible tour of the Sharon: Pelicans at an expensive lookout point Expensive lookout point in the Mishmar Hasharon reservoir

Suitable for seasons: autumn, winter, spring. Duration of the trip: about an hour depending on the level of interest. How to get there: At the Ruppin junction on Road 4, turn east onto Road 5711. We will pass the entrance to Midreshet Ruppin and at the Moshav Beit Halevi junction (c. 900 m after the entrance to Kfar Monash) we will continue to the right on the road on road 5711 (in the direction of Haniel). After 1.2 km, turn left on the road that accompanies Sderot Broshim. Drive c. 750 m and before the yellow gate, turn right onto a dirt road and at a distance of 1.7 km, carefully cross an Irish bridge over Nahal Alexander to the parking lot near the lookout. WAZE: Expensive lookout.

From the parking lot, a wide, moderately sloping road leads to the lookout point between the lawn and the ornamental garden. The lookout is a large shaded balcony above the Mishmar Hasharon reservoir built by the JNF. The reservoir absorbs the floodwaters in the Alexander River.

The large number of pelicans that spend time in the reservoir is not accidental. The common pelican population, which nests in Europe, numbers more than 30,000 individuals. This population migrates mostly and winters in Africa. All migratory individuals of this population pass through Israel during the migration seasons, mainly between September and November and between March and May. In recent years, several hundred pelicans have remained "wintering" in Israel.

Accessibility adjustments for visitors with disabilities: Disabled parking: There is no disabled parking in the parking lot. Degree of difficulty: a marked path with a moderate slope. Rest and picnic: In the lookout, 2 benches, without armrests, are built around pillars that support shading. In Tal Park next to the lookout point, picnic tables. There are no wheelchair-friendly picnic systems. The park is not wheelchair accessible. Disabled Services: There are no public services on site.

Excursion in the Yarkon Park - "Ten Mills" and the East Yarkon Promenade

An accessible trip suitable for all seasons. The length of the accessible route: about 3 km (round trip). Duration of the trip: about an hour in each direction depending on the level of interest. How to get there: On Bechor Shalom Sheetrit Street in Tel Aviv, turn to the tax parking lot. 3 - The tennis court parking lot at the National Sports Center. WAZE: Tel Aviv National Sports Center parking lot Disabled parking at the eastern end of the parking lot near the start of the route. Lowering a sidewalk at the nearby crosswalk will lead us to the accessible road to the hiking trail.

As part of the restoration of the Yarkon River, the site was cultivated and two gate buildings and a beautiful observation deck overlooking the mill waterfall located under a wooden bridge and the vegetation in the area were erected on the site. From the "Ten Mills" site, turn left on the accessible road under the Mitzpe Kadesh Street bridge to the "East Yarkon Promenade". We will continue along the accessible promenade for about 1 km to the gray bridge leading east towards Assuta Hospital. On the southern side of the promenade, the Yarkon River channel, eucalyptus trees, many benches and picnic tables. On its northern side is an extensive lawn, a fitness facilities complex and a playground facilities complex. Before the gray bridge, turn left (north) to end the route near a public toilet building and a public parking lot has escaped. Raoul Wallenberg. The way back west is the same as the way of arrival.

Accessibility accommodations for visitors with disabilities: Disabled parking: Disabled parking in the 2 parking lots. Difficulty: Wide, paved and straight road Rest and picnic: Along the route are many benches without armrests. Picnic systems: not wheelchair accessible. Disabled services: At the end of the track, a services structure that includes disabled services for women and disabled services for men. In the toilet cubicle there is a fixed handhold and a folding handhold. There is space for a wheelchair next to the toilet.
